## üìñ Site Content & Page Architecture

### 1. üè† Home
**Headline:** Welcome to the Heart of Incredible India  
**Overview:** Madhya Pradesh is not just a state; it is an experience of India in its most authentic form. Geographically located at the center of the country, it is affectionately known as the "Heart of India." From the roaring tigers of Bandhavgarh to the silence of the Sanchi Stupa, Madhya Pradesh offers a journey through time, spirituality, and untamed nature.
* **Highlight:** Home to 3 UNESCO World Heritage Sites.
* **Call to Action:** Begin your journey where history meets the wild.

### 2. üåø Explore
**Headline:** A Land of Untamed Beauty and Ancient History  
**Overview:** Uncover the hidden gems of Central India. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, Madhya Pradesh has something for you.
* **Wildlife & National Parks:** Known as the "Tiger State of India," explore the dense forests of **Kanha, Bandhavgarh, and Pench National Parks**. It is the setting that inspired Rudyard Kipling‚Äôs *The Jungle Book*.
* **Heritage Sites:** Visit the **Sanchi Stupa**, one of the oldest stone structures in India, or the **Bhimbetka Rock Shelters**, which feature prehistoric cave paintings dating back 30,000 years.
* **Forts & Palaces:** Walk through the majestic **Gwalior Fort**, described by Mughal Emperor Babur as "the pearl among fortresses in India," and explore the riverside serenity of **Maheshwar**.

### 3. üõï Temples
**Headline:** Architecture, Divinity, and Serenity  
**Overview:** Madhya Pradesh is a spiritual hub, hosting some of the most significant and architecturally stunning temples in India.
* **Khajuraho Group of Monuments:** World-famous for their intricate Nagara-style architecture and erotic sculptures, these temples are a UNESCO World Heritage site representing the balance between spiritual and physical life.
* **Mahakaleshwar (Ujjain):** One of the twelve celebrated Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. The 'Bhasma Aarti' here is a ritual that attracts devotees from across the globe.
* **Omkareshwar:** Set on an island shaped like the sacred symbol 'Om' in the Narmada River, this is a vital pilgrimage site for peace seekers.

### 4. üé≠ Festivals
**Headline:** A Celebration of Culture, Music, and Life  
**Overview:** The cultural calendar of Madhya Pradesh is vibrant and diverse. We celebrate art, dance, and tribal heritage with immense grandeur.
* **Khajuraho Dance Festival:** Held every February against the backdrop of the magnificent Khajuraho temples, this festival showcases the richness of Indian classical dance styles like Kathak, Odissi, and Bharatanatyam.
* **Tansen Samaroh (Gwalior):** A tribute to the musical legend Tansen, this festival attracts classical musicians and vocalists to perform near the tomb of the great maestro.
* **Bhagoria Haat Festival:** A vibrant tribal festival of the Bhils and Bhilalas, celebrated with colors, traditional music, and a massive fair. It signifies the end of the harvest season.
* **Lokrang:** A festival in Bhopal dedicated to performing arts and folk dances, bringing together tribal heritage and performing artists.

### 5. üçõ Cuisine
**Headline:** Flavors from the Heart of India  
**Overview:** The food in Madhya Pradesh is as diverse as its geography, ranging from the spicy curries of the north to the sweet delicacies of the Malwa region.
* **Street Food Staples:** Start your day with the famous **Poha-Jalebi** combination, a breakfast staple in Indore and Bhopal.
* **Main Courses:** Try **Dal Bafla**, a regional variation of Dal Bati, served with ghee and spicy lentils. Don't miss **Bhutte Ka Kees**, a grated corn snack tempered with spices and skimmed milk.
* **Sweets:** Indulge in **Mawa Bati** (a larger version of Gulab Jamun) and the famous **Gajak** of Morena.
* **Must-Visit:** The **Sarafa Bazaar** in Indore, a jewelry market by day that transforms into a bustling street food paradise by night.

### 6. üó£Ô∏è Languages
**Headline:** The Many Voices of Madhya Pradesh  
**Overview:** While language barriers are minimal for tourists, the linguistic landscape of MP is rich with dialects that add flavor to the culture.
* **Hindi:** The official and most widely spoken language. You will find it spoken with different accents depending on the region.
* **Regional Dialects:**
    * **Bundeli:** Spoken in the Bundelkhand region (near Jhansi/Khajuraho).
    * **Malvi:** Common in the Malwa region (Indore/Ujjain).
    * **Bagheli:** Spoken in the Baghelkhand region (Rewa/Satna).
* **Tribal Languages:** Communities like the Gonds and Bhils speak their own distinct languages, such as Gondi and Bhili, preserving an ancient oral tradition.
* **English:** Widely understood in major cities, hotels, and tourist hubs, making travel easy for international visitors.

## üåü Key Features

### üêØ Immersive Visuals
- **Cinematic Entrance:** Zoom-in parallax effects on hero images that draw the user into the landscape.
- **"Running Tiger" Integration:** A unique visual feature where a tiger animation runs across the page texture, blending seamlessly using CSS `mix-blend-mode`.

### üïâÔ∏è Heritage & Architecture ("Uncrumple" Effect)
- **Interactive Masonry Grid:** A curated display of top heritage sites like Khajuraho and Ujjain.
- **Physics-Based Modal:** Clicking a temple triggers a custom **"Uncrumple Paper" animation**. Instead of a standard pop-up, the modal expands like a crushed ball of paper smoothing out into a flat, detailed manuscript containing the history found in the "Explore" section.

### üéâ Smart Festival Calendar
- **3D Card Stack:** A visually stunning stacked slider animation for browsing cultural events like the *Khajuraho Dance Festival*.
- **Intelligent Scheduling:** The application logic highlights the next upcoming festival based on the user's current date.
